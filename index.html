About Me

Currently a senior at Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology, I have continually worked closely with STEM and computer science both in and out of school. By enrolling in advanced courses that are angled toward CS, the last four years have helped me develop and extend my programming skills. I am currently taking in Artificial Intelligence 1 and will soon be taking Artificial Intelligence 2 and Computer Vision. I am also working in my school's special Web and Mobile Research Lab, where I am developing an application for practical visualization of Git repositories.

Outside of school, I have worked as a STEM teacher assistant as part of my high school's STEM outreach program, participated in Capture the Flag programming challenges, and competed in Hackathons. I have also worked for Brain Health Alliance, a company that specializes in using computer science to find solutions to neurological problems. There, I collaborated closely with college and high school students to develop a full-stack solution to the semantic web by utilizing tools like Node.js, AngularJS, and MongoDB.

To me, programming has no limitations. There is no problem too great, no solution too far, and no idea too impossible. All that matters is the dedication and ambition that each coder has and the intrinsic drive that we carry. Currently, I have experience with both front end web programming and RDBMS database management. In the future, I want to get into software development and artifcial intelligence, hence my current classes.
